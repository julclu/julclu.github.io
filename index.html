About

- I'm a Ph.D. candidate at University of California - San Francisco in the Lupo Lab working on using machine learning in radiology to improve patient care.

- Specificallly, I am interested in creating tools that allow radiologists to more quickly and accurately diagnose hard-to-treat solid tumors. Check out my posters to learn more about the problems I work on.

- I have a diverse background in research science, with undergraduate degrees in chemistry and mathematics from the University of North Carolina - Chapel Hill.

- I have found a passion applying analytical tools to solve complex problems in medicine and radiology but am eager to apply what I've learned to new domains.

- Outside of medicine, my passions include music, education, and learning about the science of food and cooking.
