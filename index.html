I'm a Ph.D. candidate at University of California - San Francisco in the Lupo Lab working on using machine learning in radiology to improve patient care.

About

In my research, I am interested in creating tools that allow radiologists to more quickly and accurately diagnose hard-to-treat solid tumors. My project is focused on differentiating treatment-induced pseudoprogression from true recurrent glioblastoma using multi-parametric MRI techniques. I have used statistical association techniques, classical machine learning techniques such as Random Forests, and am currently working on a convolutional neural network approach. Feel free to check out my github for a peek at what I do, but sadly most is hidden in private repositories.

I have a diverse background in research science, with undergraduate degrees in chemistry and mathematics from the University of North Carolina - Chapel Hill. I have found a passion applying analytical tools to solve complex problems in medicine and radiology but am eager to apply what I've learned to new domains. Outside of medicine, my passions include music, education, and learning about the science of food and cooking.
